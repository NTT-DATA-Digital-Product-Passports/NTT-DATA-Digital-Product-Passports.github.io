Digital Data Chain

The Digital Data Chain (DDC), established by the Digital Data Chain Consortium, is a framework designed to enhance the efficiency and interoperability of data management across various sectors and organizations. It aims to streamline the flow of digital data throughout the entire value chain, facilitating secure and transparent data exchange. The DDC emphasizes key aspects such as data security, privacy, and the establishment of standardized practices for data usage. By fostering collaboration among stakeholders, the DDC seeks to unlock the full potential of data-driven insights and innovations.

Three Technologies

The digital data chain combines the three technologies:
1. Automatic identification of physical objects (IEC 61406),
2. Digital manufacturer information (VDI 2770) and
3. Digital information exchange platforms (IEP - Information Exchange Platform)
