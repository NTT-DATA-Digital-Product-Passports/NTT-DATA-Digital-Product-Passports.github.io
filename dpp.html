Digital Product Passport

The Digital Product Passport (DPP) is a transformative tool designed to provide comprehensive information about a product throughout its lifecycle. It enables transparency and traceability by documenting essential details such as materials, manufacturing processes, environmental impact, and recycling opportunities.

The DPP empowers consumers to make informed choices while encouraging manufacturers to adopt sustainable practices. By facilitating the sharing of accurate data among stakeholders, the DPP aims to support a circular economy, reduce waste, and promote responsible consumption, ultimately contributing to a more sustainable future.

Sustainable Products Regulation

The EU Digital Product Passport (DPP) is a regulatory initiative aimed at enhancing product transparency and traceability throughout their lifecycle. Starting in 2024, it will be mandatory for nearly all products sold in the EU to feature a DPP, which serves as a digital record containing key traceability data about the product. The DPP is part of the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR), promoting a circular economy by tracking comprehensive lifecycle data—from material sourcing to disposal
